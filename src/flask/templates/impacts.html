The bigger picture

COVID-19 is affecting us in a variety of ways. Here are some examples, and how you can get support if you're struggling.

Over 5 million people have been diagnosed with depression in the UK.

During the pandemic, as people spend more time alone and away from friends we need to be more aware of how how we can support each other.

Mind

This is a mental health emergency. Help us reach more people feeling trapped, isolated and alone with our peer support networks and advice on staying well.

Visit Mind

There were over 1 million reported incidents of domestic abuse in 2018/19.

Some of the most vulnerable people in our society are at greater risk now they cannot leave their home. It is important they receive the support, understanding and treatment they deserve.

Help and advice

The National Center for Domestic Violence are a free, fast emergency injunction service to survivors of domestic violence regardless of their financial circumstances, race, gender or sexual orientation.

Visit NCDV

1.8 million people are registered as having chronic heart disease.

Heart disease is the single most common cause of premature death in the UK.
You can support research to develop cures and treatments for these chronic conditions.

British Heart Foundation

Our vision is a world free from the fear of heart and circulatory diseases. We raise money to research cures and treatments, so we can beat heartbreak forever.

Visit BHF

Loneliness is a real issue.

Over 2.5 million people reported feeling lonely "often/always", according to the ONS 2016/17 Community Life Survey. We all need somebody to talk to, especially in troubling times.

Supportline

SupportLine is particularly aimed at those who are isolated, at risk, vulnerable and victims of any form of abuse. They provide a confidential telephone helpline offering emotional support to any individual on any issue.

Supportline

Mental health problems affect 1 in 4 of us every year.

Nobody should feel ashamed. By sharing our experiences together, we can end the stigma.
Checking in with friends, family and colleagues is more important than ever during the coronavirus outbreak.
