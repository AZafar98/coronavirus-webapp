This graph allows you to visualise COVID-19 data across the world.

For example, to see the trend in how many new cases the UK is reporting each day choose the 'New reports per day' option with 'Confirmed cases' and 'United Kingdom'.

You can also compare data between countries to to see how long it took each country to reach it's current level of cases, for example. To compare the US and China, choose 'Total reports' , 'Confirmed cases' and 'US, China'.

All of the COVID-19 data is sourced directly from the John Hopkins University and Medicine Github Repository. For more information about the data, see the about page.

How does this compare?

We understand the seriousness of the threat COVID-19 poses, and we do not want to take anything away from that.

Instead, we want to take this as an opportunity to reflect on the wider issues that we as a society face everyday and in particular, draw your attention to the level of loneliness, depression and domestic violence in the UK.

In offering this perspective, we hope this can encourage individuals to come together to combat these issues in a similar way to how we are collectively fighting COVID-19.

Depression
The number of people recorded as suffering from depression by their GP as of 2018/19. Depression can affect any one of us. We should aim to be more understanding and aware of it.
Domestic Abuse
The number of reported incidents of domestic violence in 2018/19. Tackling domestic abuse as a public health issue is vital for ensuring that some of the most vulnerable people in our society receive the support, understanding and treatment they deserve.
Heart Disease
The number of patients with a registered Coronary heart disease (CHD) in the year 2018/19. CHD is the single most common cause of premature death in the UK.
Loneliness
The number of people who reported feeling lonely "often/always". Loneliness is considered by many to be one of the largest health risks we face. It is important we tackle this, and it is something we can all play our part in.
About the data used
A note about the data...

The COVID-19 data is as-compiled by the John Hopkins University. These figures are not a true representaion of the scale of the COVID-19 outbreak as they only represent the instances where a positive test is present.

The data is sourced from PHE and the ONS.
Data from PHE has been sourced using the fingertips_py Python package.
The ONS data has been taken from reports and thus is not directly accessible to the user.

Heart Disease (PHE)

The percentage of patients with coronary heart disease, as recorded on practice disease register "Coronary heart disease (CHD) is the single most common cause of premature death in the UK. The research evidence relating to the management of CHD is well established and if implemented can reduce the risk of death from CHD and improve the quality of life for patients. This indicator set focuses on the management of patients with established CHD consistent with clinical priorities in the four nations."
PHE Fingertips Indicator ID 273

Depression (PHE)

The percentage of patients aged 18 and over with depression, as recorded on practice disease registers.CCG: The recorded depression prevalence is the number of people with depression recorded on their practice register within a CCG, as a proportion of the practice list size of the CCG aged 18 years or over
