About

We’ve listed some frequently asked questions and useful info for you.

COVID-19 is the greatest threat most of us have seen in our lifetimes. It is going to affect all of us, and in the most part, it will affect us negatively.

However, amongst this we see an opportunity to ignite some lasting change going forwards.

There are record numbers of people suffering from depression, loneliness and domestic abuse. These numbers have been growing steadily, and are only likely to increase with lockdown measures in place.

The collective response and willingness to fight COVID-19 as one has been incredible. We believe that if we could transfer even a fraction of this into tackling other important issues facing the UK, then we could see genuine change soon.

The first step is to spread awareness of just how prevalent issues such as depression and domestic violence are and we hope that this will open your mind to the issues and encourage you to take action.

We believe open source is a force for good. It inspires and drives the world of technology and can bring about real innovation.

In this spirit, all the code for this webapp is available on GitHub so you can build your own COVID-19 related tools! Comments, suggestions and improvements are welcomed.

Interested in the design process? Check out our designers Figma profile!

COVID-19 Data

We have tried to visualise the worldwide COVID-19 data in a simple and easy to understand way.

In order to better understand the pandemic and it's progression, it is useful to look at both the total number of reported cases in a country, and also the trend in new reported cases/deaths per day.

If the number of new reports per day is still increasing, it is likely that the country has not yet reached the worst of the pandemic. Once this curve starts to decline it means that less people were infected today than yesterday. A continuing downward trend, therefore, can suggest that the pandemic is better under control.

Public Health Data

We have displayed a selection of other data relating to public health issues in the UK. We are not suggesting this is compared to COVID-19 directly.

The data is meant to offer perspective and make you aware of the scale of these issues. We want you to understand why it is important to tackle these issues, and for you to spread the knowledge and message.

Data types

Total Reports: The total number of reports in the country each day. This is an cumulative measure.

New reports per day: The number of new reports each day. I.e. totalReportsToday - totalReportsYesterday gives the number of new reports today.

Data categories

Confirmed cases: The number of confirmed COVID-19 cases in a country. This is generally defined as the number of people who have been tested for COVID-19 and returned a positive test.

Deaths: The number of deaths attributed to COVID-19 in a country. This is generally defined as the number of people who have died and tested positive for COVID-19.

Recovered cases: The number of confirmed recoveries from COVID-19 in a country. This is generally defined as the number of people who have tested positive for COVID-19 and subsequently recovered.

The COVID-19 data is taken directly from the John Hopkins CSSE.

The public health data is from Public Health England and the Office for National Statistics. Click the button below for detailed information about the data.

Note that the data is updated at 01:00 BST daily.

About the data used
A note about the data...

The COVID-19 data is as-compiled by the John Hopkins University. These figures are not a true representaion of the scale of the COVID-19 outbreak as they only represent the instances where a positive test is present.

The data is sourced from PHE and the ONS.
Data from PHE has been sourced using the fingertips_py Python package.
The ONS data has been taken from reports and thus is not directly accessible to the user.
